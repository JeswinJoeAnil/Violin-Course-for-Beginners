Finger Placement Drill

Complete Beginner Guide to Sevcik Op. 1, Part 1

Before You Start

🎯 Goal of This Exercise

By the end of this drill, you should be able to place fingers cleanly without tension, keep unused fingers down, and maintain a stable hand frame. This drill trains:

  • Develop finger independence
  • Improve pitch accuracy (intonation)
  • Build correct left-hand shape
  • Train muscle memory for first position
  • Reduce tension while playing

🧠 Before You Start (Critical Setup)

Important: Your setup determines how effective this exercise will be. Never skip posture and hand position basics.

📷 Correct Thumb Placement

✋ Left Hand Position
  • Keep the hand relaxed — never squeeze the neck
  • Thumb should rest lightly opposite the first or second finger
  • Fingers must be curved, not flat
  • Use fingertip pads, not nails
  • Imagine holding a small ball in your hand
📏 Wrist Alignment
  • Wrist must remain straight
  • Avoid collapsing inward or pushing outward
  • Forearm, wrist, and hand should form one straight line
✅ Correct Wrist Alignment
Straight line from arm to hand
❌ Incorrect Wrist Alignment
Avoid collapsing inward/outward
The Routine

🎼 The Exercise (Step-by-Step)

1️⃣ Step 1: Initial Placement

Start on the A string in first position. Place all four fingers down at once. Keep all fingers down initially.

Finger Note (A String) Placement
0 (Open) A No finger
1st Finger B Close to nut
2nd Finger C# Close to 1st finger
3rd Finger D Slight gap
4th Finger E Next to 3rd

🔁 Finger Lifting Pattern

Lift only one finger at a time. Keep other fingers pressed down. Follow this order:

  1. Lift 1 → place
  2. Lift 2 → place
  3. Lift 3 → place
  4. Lift 4 → place

Repeat this pattern slowly.

👆 Finger Action (Tap Technique)
  • Each finger should land with a light tap
  • Do not press hard
  • Use minimum pressure required for clean sound
  • Avoid tension buildup
📷 Correct Finger Shape
Curved fingers & raised knuckles
❌ Collapsed Fingers
Avoid flat fingers
Mastery

🔁 Practice Routine & Pitfalls

⏱️ Daily Plan
  • Practice on A, D, and G strings
  • Spend about 10 minutes total
  • Play very slowly
  • Focus on accuracy over speed
⚠️

Common Mistakes

  • Lifting multiple fingers together
  • Pressing too hard
  • Flat fingers
  • Thumb squeezing the neck
  • Collapsed wrist
🧠

Pro Tips

  • Keep fingers close to the string
  • Practice in front of a mirror
  • Use a tuner to check pitch
  • Stay relaxed at all times
✅ Self-Check
  • Fingers are curved
  • Knuckles are raised
  • Wrist is straight
  • Only one finger moves at a time
  • Hand is relaxed
