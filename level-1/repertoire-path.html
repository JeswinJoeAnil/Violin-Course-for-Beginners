Level I: Repertoire Path

The musical milestones of your beginner journey

Your Journey

The Road to Mastery

The repertoire in Level 1 is carefully selected from the Suzuki Method (Books 1 & 2) and traditional graded systems. Each piece is not just a song; it is a vehicle to teach a specific new technical skill. Do not skip pieces, even if they seem simple.

The Catalog

Essential Pieces

Piece Composer Core Technique Focus Milestone Goal
Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star (Var. A) Shinichi Suzuki Rhythm, Staccato bow strokes, basic A String/E String string crossings. Mastering the 'stop-bow' technique.
Lightly Row Folk Song Détaché bowing, playing multiple notes on a single string cleanly. Developing a smooth, connected tone.
Song of the Wind Folk Song Quick string crossings, retaking the bow (circle bow). Agility and right-hand recovery.
O Come, Little Children Folk Song Up-beat entrances, slurs (two notes in one bow). Bow distribution and planning.
Minuet 1 & 2 J.S. Bach Musical phrasing, dynamics (loud vs soft), classical style, 4th finger usage. Playing with actual musical expression.
Chorus from Judas Maccabaeus G.F. Handel Grand, broad tone. Long sustained notes in 4/4 meter. Sustained tone production.
Hunters' Chorus (Book 2) C.M. von Weber Advanced string crossings, hooked bowing. Right arm flexibility.
Two Grenadiers (Book 2) R. Schumann Playing in Minor keys (low 2nd finger pattern), dramatic expression. Intonation in a new finger pattern.
Musette (Book 2) J.S. Bach D Major scale patterns, agility, grace notes. Graduation from Level 1!
