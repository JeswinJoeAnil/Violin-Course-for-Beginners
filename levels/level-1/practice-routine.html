Daily Practice Routine

How to structure your 60-minute session for maximum progress

The Philosophy

Quality Over Quantity

Practicing making mistakes makes you perfect at playing mistakes. The goal of daily practice is conscious, deliberate repetition of correct technique. One hour of deeply focused practice will always outperform three hours of mindless repetition while watching TV.

The Blueprint

The 60-Minute Power Hour

🧘

1. Preparation (5 Min)

Before making a sound. Tuning the instrument, rosining the bow. Windshield Wiper and Spider Crawl exercises for the right hand.

🪶

2. Tone Production (10 Min)

Long, slow bows on open strings. Focus entirely on pulling a straight bow, maintaining a consistent contact point, and creating a resonant, scratch-free sound.

🎼

3. Left Hand Technique (15 Min)

Sevcik Op. 1 exercises and scales. Practice placing fingers cleanly (the "tap") without squeezing the neck. Ensure intonation is precise using a tuner drone.

🎻

4. Repertoire & Pieces (25 Min)

Practicing your Suzuki pieces. Do not just play through. Isolate the hardest 2 measures, slow them down, and repeat them correctly 5 times in a row before moving on.

🧠

5. Theory & Cool Down (5 Min)

Reviewing sheet music, naming notes mentally, clapping rhythms. Ending the session on a positive, relaxed note to relieve physical tension.

Warning Signs

When to Stop

⚠️ Physical Pain

Aching muscles (like after the gym) are normal for beginners. Sharp pain, tingling, or numbness are NOT. If you feel sharp pain in your wrist, neck, or shoulder, stop immediately. Your set up or posture needs adjustment.

🤯 Mental Fatigue

If you find yourself making the same mistake 4 times in a row without knowing why, your brain is full. Put the violin down, take a 10-minute break, drink some water, and come back to it. Frustrated practice is anti-practice.
