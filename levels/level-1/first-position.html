Geography Basics

What is First Position?

"First Position" refers to the hand placement closest to the scroll (the nut) of the violin. 90% of beginner repertoire is played entirely in First Position. In this position, the first finger rests approximately an inch from the nut, and the hand covers an interval of a perfect fourth on each string.

Scale Patterns

Major Finger Patterns

1️⃣ Pattern 1: High 2nd Finger

Used for D Major and A Major scales.

In this pattern, there is a whole step between the 1st and 2nd fingers, and a half step between the 2nd and 3rd fingers. This means the 2nd and 3rd fingers touch.

  • Finger 1  ← whole step →  Finger 2
  • Finger 2  ← HALF STEP →  Finger 3 (touching!)
  • Finger 3  ← whole step →  Finger 4
2️⃣ Pattern 2: Low 2nd Finger

Used for G Major and C Major scales.

In this pattern, there is a half step between the 1st and 2nd fingers. This means the 1st and 2nd fingers touch.

  • Finger 1  ← HALF STEP →  Finger 2 (touching!)
  • Finger 2  ← whole step →  Finger 3
  • Finger 3  ← whole step →  Finger 4
