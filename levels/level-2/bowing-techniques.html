Advanced Bow Articulation

From Martelé to Spiccato: Controlling the nuanced syntax of string playing.

Part 1

The Martelé Stroke

Martelé ("hammered") is the fundamental articulation that builds right-hand finger strength. Every stroke requires preparation: apply weight into the string while the bow is stopped (the "bite"), move the bow quickly, and release the weight simultaneously to produce a bell-like ringing resonance.

Part 2

Introducing Spiccato

🏹 The Bouncing Bow

Spiccato is an off-the-string stroke used for fast, crisp passages. Unlike ricochet, every single bounce is actively controlled by the player. It is typically executed around the balance point of the bow.

⚙️ Mechanics

According to Ivan Galamian, spiccato is essentially a fast détaché stroke that is allowed to leave the string due to the natural elasticity of the bow hair and stick. The initial dropping motion is shaped like a "U" or "V".

Part 3

Bowing Polyphony: Double Stops

Playing two strings simultaneously requires absolute precision in arm angle. The arm elevation must be perfectly centered between the two string planes.

📐 The Rule of String Levels

There are 7 distinct right-arm elevations on the violin: G string alone, G&D double stop, D string alone, D&A double stop, A string alone, A&E double stop, E string alone. Practice finding these levels silently.
