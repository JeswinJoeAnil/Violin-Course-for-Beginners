Repertoire Progression

The standard conservatory transition from student pieces directly into the lower classical masterworks.

Part 1

The Student Concerti

Before tackling Mozart or Mendelssohn, students must master the "Student Concerti." These pieces were written specifically by 19th-century violin masters to bridge the gap in position playing, bowing variety, and musical structure.

Part 2

Core Milestones

Piece & Composer Primary Technical Challenge Musical Goal
Rieding: Concerto in B minor, Op. 35 Complete mastery of 1st position with basic martele. Maintaining tempo and producing commanding tone.
Seitz: Concerto No.2 or No.5 Introduction to 3rd position, shifting while bowing. Classical phrasing, sonata form structure.
Vivaldi: Concerto in A minor, Op.3 No.6 Fast string crossings, baroque detachment. Rhythmic vitality, absolute metronomic timing.
Accolay: Concerto No.1 in A minor Positions 1 through 5, continuous vibrato, heavy string crossings. Romantic period expression, the dramatic solo entrance.
Fiocco: Allegro Relentless fast detaché and early spiccato. Stamina, right-hand relaxation at high speed.
Part 3

Graduating Level II

The Final Test: The typical capstone for Level II is the Bach Double Concerto in D minor (First Violin part) or the opening movement of Mozart's Concerto No. 3 in G major. Successfully performing either of these pieces from memory signifies readiness for Level III (Advanced).
