Level II: Intermediate Skills → 2.5 Practice Routine

The 2-Hour Routine

A structured, Juilliard-style practice template designed for rapid intermediate progression.

Part 1

Structure & Philosophy

At the intermediate level, mindless repetition becomes actively harmful. You must employ the "divide and conquer" strategy. Never perform in the practice room. Practice is about isolating problems, breaking them down into tiny solvable mechanics, and reassembling them.

Part 2

The Standard 2-Hour Block

⏱️ Block 1: Mechanics (40 min)
  • 5m: Open string tone drills (son filé).
  • 15m: Scales and Arpeggios. 3-octave major/minor using Galamian rhythms.
  • 10m: Shifting drills (Sevcik Op. 8).
  • 10m: Vibrato focused exercises (rhythmic metronome rolling).
⏱️ Block 2: Études (30 min)
  • Kreutzer or Mazas. Treat études as pure technical medicine. Do not focus on musicality initially. Diagnose the specific mechanical challenge (e.g. crossing strings, martele) and hyper-focus on solving it.
⏱️ Block 3: Repertoire (50 min)
  • The 80/20 Rule: Spend 80% of your time on the hardest 20% of the piece.
  • Tempo building: Use a metronome. Start at 50% of target tempo. Increment by 4 BPM only when you can play the passage 3 times perfectly in a row.
  • Musicality: Spend the final 10 minutes making music. Record yourself.
Part 3

Pro-Tip: Interleaved Practice

The Exercise: Instead of practicing one passage for 30 minutes straight (blocked practice), rotate between three difficult passages every 5-7 minutes (interleaved practice). Science shows this dramatically improves long-term retention, even if it feels harder in the moment.
