The Deliberate Practice Framework

🧠 Ericsson's Deliberate Practice Principles
  1. Work at the edge of your ability — always practice what is just beyond comfortable.
  2. Immediate feedback loop — record every session, or use a mirror.
  3. Isolate and drill — extract the difficult 4 measures. Practice only those for 30 minutes.
  4. Slow → Fast — all new material begins at 50% of target tempo.
  1. Mental practice — 15 minutes of silent score study per day activates the same neural pathways as physical practice.
  2. Rest is practice — the brain consolidates motor memories during sleep.
  3. Galamian Rhythmic Variations — practice in dotted rhythms before playing evenly.
  4. Practice the transitions — the bar before and after a difficulty is as important as the difficulty itself.
P.2

Weekly Progression Plan

Monday
Technique Heavy. Full scale system, Sevcik/Galamian variations, double stops. New étude — learn notes only.
Tuesday
Repertoire Focus. Slow practice of current concerto — one movement, phrase by phrase.
Wednesday
Mixed + Theory. Scale system (shorter), étude refinement, music theory study (45 min), ear training (30 min).
Thursday
Technique + New Material. Full bowing exercises. Introduce next section of concerto. Sight-reading (30 min).
Friday
Performance Simulation. Play through all repertoire without stopping. Treat as a concert. Record the session.
Saturday
Analysis + Fix. Listen to Friday's recording. Identify 3 specific problems. Practice only those problems.
Sunday
Rest or Light Play. Optional: 30 min gentle scales, listen to recordings by Heifetz, Oistrakh, Hilary Hahn, Maxim Vengerov.
